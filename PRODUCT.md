# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters and hiring managers evaluating Cole Turner for a role. They arrive with limited time and are scanning for relevant experience, skills, and projects — not reading every word. The job to be done is fast qualification: does this person seem competent, memorable, and worth an interview?

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio site that helps Cole land interviews and job offers. It presents background, projects, skills, experience, and education through an ERAU baseball scoreboard concept (side-panel navigation, live GitHub activity strip). Success looks like a recruiter forming a positive, distinct impression quickly and taking action (reaching out, moving to next round).

## Brand Personality

Approachable, curious, hardworking. The scoreboard/baseball framing carries discipline and competitive precision as a structural device, but the tone throughout should stay warm and grounded — someone who learns on the fly and puts in the work, not a hard-edged "competitive athlete" persona.

## Anti-references

Avoid the generic AI/template-generated developer portfolio look: identical card grids, gradient hero text, stock icon rows, tiny uppercase eyebrows over every section, hero-metric templates. The scoreboard concept should stay the load-bearing idea — don't dilute it with boilerplate SaaS-portfolio scaffolding.

## Design Principles

- Show, don't tell — the scoreboard structure and live GitHub data should demonstrate initiative and technical ability rather than asserting it in prose.
- Fast qualification — recruiters skim; hierarchy must surface the most relevant signal (relevant experience, standout projects) within seconds.
- Substance over cliché — favor real, functional detail (live stats, concrete project outcomes) over decorative filler.
- One coherent metaphor — the baseball/scoreboard concept is the brand's distinct identity; every section should extend it rather than default back to generic portfolio patterns.
- Warm, not cold precision — discipline and rigor show up in craft and structure, not in a stiff or overly corporate voice.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA baseline: sufficient color contrast against the navy scoreboard palette, full keyboard navigation between side-panel sections, and `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for any scoreboard/panel transitions.
